Average Speed

When an object moves along in a straight line at a steady speed, you can calculate its average speed if you know how far it travels and how long it takes.The following equation shows the relationship between the average speed, distance moved and time taken:

Equation: average-speed = distance-moved/time taken

where average speed is measured in metres per second,m/s distance moves is measured in metres,m time taken is measured in seconds,s.

For Example,a car travels 300m in 20 s.Its average speed is 300/20 = 15 m/s

Question
A man runs after a bus.The bus is travelling at an average speed of 5m/s.The man runs 25m in 6s.Does he catch the bus ?

Reveal Answer

To calculate the motion of everyday objects such as toy cars or tennis balls: measure the distance that the object travels in metres measures the time it takes for the object to travel that distance use the equation given above to calculate the average speed of the object.
