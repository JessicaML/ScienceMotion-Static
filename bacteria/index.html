Bacteria

Bacteria are microscopic single-celled organisms. Bacteria have a number of shapes, ranging from spheres to rods and spirals. Bacteria inhabit soil, water, acidic hot springs, radioactive waste, and the deep portions of Earth's crust. Following are few examples of various bacteria that we encounter in pur everyday life:

  1. Escherichia coli
    • _ A single bacterium
    • _ Rod-shaped bacteria
    • _ Genus: Salmonella
    • _ Also called E-coli bacteria
    • _ Commonly found in the intestines of animals

  2. Lactobacillus bulgaricus
    • _ Rod-shaped bacterium
    • _ Used to make yoghurt from milk

  3. Pneumococcus
    • _ A spherical bacterium
    • _ Acts as the pathogen that causes pneumonia

Bacterial Cell Structure

Bacterial cells have a cell wall made of polysaccharides and proteins.
They do not have a nucleus, but instead they have a circular chromosome of DNA. They may also have small extra circles of DNA called plasmids.

Other Features of Bacteria

Some bacteria can carry out photosynthesis, but most bacteria feed from other organisms (living or dead).
Some bacteria are pathogens, for example Pneumococcus (which causes pneumonia).
