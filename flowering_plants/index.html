Characteristics of living organisms

Flowering Plants

Flowering plants can be classified according to the nature of their seeds, seedlings and leaves. The two largest groups of flowering plants are:
monocotyledons
eudicotyledons (also called dicotyledons)

Eudicotyledons

Sunflower plants
Sunflower plants are eudicotyledons The seeds of eudicotyledons each contain two embryonic leaves. Eudicotyledons have leaves with branching veins, and the parts of their flowers come in fours or fives. Examples of eudicotyledons include:
buttercups
dandelions
oak trees
